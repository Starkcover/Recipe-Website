Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

    For the Gulab Jamun:
  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 cup milk (you may need a little more or less)
  • Oil or ghee for frying
    For the Sugar Syrup:
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • A few drops of lemon juice

Recipe

Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a large saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Heat the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves, stirring occasionally. Add the crushed cardamom pods, saffron strands (if using), and lemon juice. The lemon juice prevents the syrup from crystallizing. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes, until slightly thickened. Turn off the heat and add rose water (if using). Set aside. Make the Dough for Gulab Jamun: In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add the ghee and mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add milk and knead to form a smooth, soft dough. The dough should not be too dry or too sticky. Let the dough rest for about 10 minutes. Shape and Fry the Gulab Jamun: Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls. Ensure there are no cracks on the surface. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over medium-low heat. Fry the dough balls in batches, stirring gently to ensure even cooking. Fry until they turn golden brown. Remove the fried Gulab Jamun and drain on paper towels. Soak in Sugar Syrup: While the Gulab Jamun are still warm, transfer them to the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the syrup and become soft and flavorful. Serve: Serve the Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature. They can also be garnished with chopped nuts if desired.



Dosa

Ingredients

    For the Dosa Batter:
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt to taste
    For Cooking:
  • Oil or ghee (clarified butter)

Recipe

Preparation: Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately until the water runs clear. Soak the rice and urad dal with fenugreek seeds in separate bowls of water for about 4-6 hours or overnight. Grinding the Batter: Drain the water from the soaked rice and dal. Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds first in a mixer or wet grinder to a smooth paste, adding water as needed. Next, grind the rice to a slightly coarse paste, adding water as needed. Combine both pastes in a large bowl, mix well, and add salt. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight until it has doubled in volume and has a slightly sour aroma. Making the Dosa: Heat a non-stick or cast iron tawa (griddle) on medium heat. Lightly grease the tawa with oil or ghee. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the tawa and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin crepe. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the edges start to lift and the dosa turns golden brown and crispy. Fold the dosa and remove it from the tawa. Serving: Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any of your favorite accompaniments.



Biryani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (or vegetables)
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Saffron strands (soaked in milk)
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

Recipe

Preparation: Rinse and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Cook until 70% done and set aside. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add chicken or vegetables and cook until browned. Add tomatoes, yogurt, biryani masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook until oil separates. Layer cooked rice over the chicken or vegetables. Sprinkle fresh coriander, mint leaves, and saffron milk on top. Cover and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes. Serve hot with raita.



Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add paneer cubes and mix gently. Cook for 5-7 minutes. Add cream and mix well. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan or rice.



Butter Chicken

Ingredients

    For the Marinade:
  • 500g chicken (boneless)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste
    For the Gravy:
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Marinate the chicken with yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Heat butter in a pan. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add marinated chicken and cook until done. Add cream and mix well. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan or rice.



Rajma (Kidney Bean Curry)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Soak kidney beans overnight. Pressure cook with water and salt until tender. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add cooked kidney beans and simmer for 15-20 minutes. Adjust salt and spices as needed. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice or roti.



Aloo Gobi (Potato and Cauliflower)

Ingredients

  • 2 potatoes (cubed)
  • 1 small cauliflower (cut into florets)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomatoes and cook until soft. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add potatoes and cauliflower, and cook until they are tender. Adjust salt and spices as needed. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with roti or rice.

Palak Paneer (Spinach and Cottage Cheese)

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 500g spinach leaves (palak)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cream (optional, for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Blanch spinach leaves in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then immediately transfer to ice water. Drain and blend into a smooth paste. Heat oil in a pan. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add spinach paste and cook for 5-7 minutes. Add paneer cubes and cook until heated through. Adjust salt and spices as needed. Garnish with fresh cream if desired. Serve hot with naan or rice.

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

Preparation: Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell goes away. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Add chicken pieces and cook until done. Adjust salt and spices as needed. Simmer for 10-15 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice or roti.
